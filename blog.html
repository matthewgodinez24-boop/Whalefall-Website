April 3, 2026

Notes from the coast

We tracked most of For You, to Blame in February, in a small room with the windows open because the heater made too much noise. You can hear it on the record if you listen close—the room, I mean. The walls. A car that passes around the second chorus and never comes back.

The song took a long time to settle. It started as something else entirely—a different tempo, a different key, a different person singing it. Most of what ended up on the record was written in the last week before we recorded, in the car on the way up the 101. There's a stretch near Jenner where the road bends inland and the cliffs drop off and you can see the whole coast for about thirty seconds before it closes back up. The second verse came from there. Or from trying to remember it afterward.

We didn't talk about what the song was about until after it was done. I still don't know, entirely. Maybe that's the point, or maybe I just haven't gotten there yet.

More soon. Quieter this time, probably.

February 18, 2026

On playing small rooms

Played a house show in Oakland last week—maybe forty people, living room, folding chairs borrowed from a church down the street. We unplugged for most of it. It's strange how much louder a quiet song gets when nobody's drinking a beer over it. You can hear the floorboards. You can hear someone's cat in the next room.

I used to think we needed bigger rooms. I don't anymore. The songs we've been writing want to be heard at a certain volume, and a certain distance. They want the listener inside the room, not across it.

We've been talking about doing more of these. If you have a living room or a back porch that could fit twenty or thirty people, and the neighbors are the forgiving kind, tell us.

November 27, 2025

Four months off the record

Haven't written here in a while. We spent most of October and November not playing—not touring, not recording, just writing. It felt necessary. The things we made before were getting too loud in our heads, and we couldn't hear what was underneath.

There's a pressure, in this part of the world, to keep releasing. To keep being visible. A song every six weeks. A post every day. We felt it too. We don't think we write well inside that pressure, so we stepped out of it for a while, and we drove up the coast instead, and we didn't bring the good mics.

Back in January with something. Maybe sooner. Maybe not.
